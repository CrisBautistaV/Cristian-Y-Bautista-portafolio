Rabbit hOle project

Experimental platform

Rabbit hOle is an evolving platform for exploring how human movement, sound, and computation interact in real time. It brings together sensing, generative systems, and audiovisual feedback into a shared environment where interaction becomes the central element. As an ecosystem, it involves live performance, workshops and installations.

The project is a space for developing and testing ideas around music/sound, perception, embodiment, and responsive systems, not a single focused application.

Status: active development — expanding through independent experiments

Motive

For years, engineering and artistic creation evolved as parallel paths in my work. Rabbit hOle emerges as a point of convergence: a system where sensors, computer vision, and real-time sound synthesis become tools to expand musical experience without losing rhythm, structure, or presence.

From live performance to interactive installations and open experimentation spaces, the project explores accessible and immersive forms of interaction. It is an ongoing search for new territories in the relationship between sound, movement, and electronic systems.

System

Rabbit hOle is structured as a modular system where different technologies can be combined and reconfigured depending on the experiment.

  • Human movement tracking (MediaPipe, IMU, ESP32)
  • Generative sound systems (Pure Data, Python)
  • Real-time data interaction and feedback
  • Explorations in perception and embodiment

Experiments

Current explorations focus on how input data from the body can shape sound and structure:

  • Movement-driven sound generation
  • Markov-based musical structures
  • Sensor-based interaction systems
  • Audio-reactive environments
