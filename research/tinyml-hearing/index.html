TinyML device for people with hearing loss

Edge-based sound recognition with haptic feedback for situational awareness

A portable TinyML device designed to assist people with hearing impairment by detecting critical environmental sounds and translating them into haptic feedback. The system operates entirely on-device, enabling real-time awareness without reliance on external infrastructure.

Motive

People with hearing loss may not perceive critical auditory cues in their environment, especially in situations requiring immediate attention. This project explores how embedded machine learning can provide an alternative sensory channel through touch.

The goal was to create a discreet and portable system capable of identifying relevant sounds and conveying both their presence and direction, enhancing situational awareness in everyday contexts.

Method

The system combines embedded hardware design with optimized machine learning models for execution on resource-constrained devices.

  • Audio acquisition using MEMS microphones
  • Feature extraction via FFT and Mel-frequency representation
  • Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for sound classification
  • Model optimization for TinyML deployment
  • Direction of Arrival (DOA) estimation
  • Haptic feedback through patterned vibration

Results

The system in the shape of earphones, successfully identifies four types of emergency sounds: car horn, scream, ambulance siren, and crying baby, providing distinct vibration patterns for each category plus different intensity according to the DOA.

The model achieves 77.82% classification accuracy with an inference time of approximately 0.08 seconds, fitting within 147 KB of Flash memory and 25 KB of RAM, demonstrating feasibility for real-time edge deployment.

Publication

Bautista-Villalpando, C., Lomas-Barrie, V. A TinyML device for risk identification for people with hearing loss Internet of Things, Volume 35, 2026.
