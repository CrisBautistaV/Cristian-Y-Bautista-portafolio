Patient Segmentation

Hierarchical clustering for breast cancer patient analysis

A machine learning study focused on identifying meaningful patient groups through unsupervised learning techniques. The project explores how clustering methods can reveal patterns in clinical and psychosocial data, with the objective of leading to the most significant medical attention needed at that moment, thus avoiding overwhelming and saving time and resources.

Motive

Patient populations are often treated as homogeneous groups, despite significant variability in clinical, psychological, and contextual factors. This project investigates how data-driven segmentation can provide a more nuanced understanding of patient profiles.

The goal is to explore whether clustering techniques can uncover interpretable groupings that may support more personalized approaches to care and analysis.

Method

The analysis is based on hierarchical clustering, applied to a dataset containing multi-dimensional features related to patient condition and context.

  • Data preprocessing and cleaning
  • Handling of categorical and numerical variables
  • Feature encoding and normalization
  • Hierarchical clustering (agglomerative approach)
  • Evaluation using clustering metrics and interpretability

Results

The clustering process revealed distinct patient groups with differentiable characteristics, suggesting that meaningful segmentation can emerge from combined clinical and non-clinical variables, highlighting the most relevant features for patient profiling.

This approach is intended to be applied in the coming months, and be able to evaluate if this patient profiling method is accurate to advise the most significant medical references needed in a patience.
